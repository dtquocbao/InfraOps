## 1. Site Overview

Substation Alpha is a greenfield 138/13.8 kV switching and transformation facility located on a 4.2-acre parcel at Helix Power Cooperative's Ridgeview Industrial Corridor. The layout follows a breaker-and-a-half configuration on the 138 kV side, with two main power transformers (MPT-1 and MPT-2) rated 50 MVA each, ONAN/ONAF cooling. The 13.8 kV distribution bus is arranged in a ring configuration to support future feeder expansion.

## 2. Equipment Placement

The 138 kV GIS switchyard occupies the northern 60% of the fenced yard. Bus sections A and B are separated by a 45-foot maintenance aisle compliant with IEEE 693 moderate seismic requirements. Control building CB-01 (24 ft × 48 ft) is positioned on the south boundary, 85 feet from the nearest live 138 kV equipment to satisfy arc-flash boundary calculations per NFPA 70E.

Underground duct banks route from the control building to each transformer bay via two 6-inch PVC conduits with spare capacity for fiber and SCADA. The oil containment system for MPT-1 and MPT-2 includes 110% capacity concrete bund walls with leak detection tied to the SCADA alarm panel.

## 3. Clearances and Access

Minimum phase-to-ground clearance at 138 kV is 3.6 feet per NESC Table 235-5. Crane access for transformer delivery requires a 30-foot-wide gravel pad along the east perimeter. Fire department access road width is 24 feet with a minimum turning radius of 45 feet.
