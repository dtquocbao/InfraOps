## 1. Scope

This specification covers the design, procurement, and installation of the 138 kV transmission line segment connecting Helix Power Cooperative's existing Westfield Substation to Substation Alpha. Total route length is 2.8 miles (14,784 feet), consisting of 38 self-supporting steel monopoles and two dead-end structures at each substation terminal.

## 2. Conductor and Hardware

Phase conductors shall be 795 kcmil ACSR "Drake" (26/7) with a rated breaking strength of 31,500 lbs. Shield wire shall be 7 No. 8 AWG alumoweld with a fiber-optic composite (OPGW) for SCADA backhaul. Minimum conductor sag at 60°F and 0.5 in/ft wind loading shall not exceed 18 feet at maximum span of 850 feet.

Insulators shall be polymer long-rod type, 15 kV per unit, with a minimum string length of 78 inches for 138 kV operating voltage (nominal 145 kV BIL). Hardware shall be hot-dip galvanized per ASTM A153.

## 3. Structural Requirements

Pole structures shall be designed per NESC Grade B loading: combined ice (0.5 inch) and wind (4 psf). Foundation type varies by soil report SR-2026-014: direct embedment for Class II soils, drilled pier foundations (48-inch diameter, 14-foot depth) for Class IV soils identified at milepost 1.2 through 1.6.

Grounding at each structure includes two 5/8-inch × 10-foot copper-clad ground rods with a maximum resistance of 25 ohms per structure, tested per IEEE 81.

## 4. Testing and Commissioning

Prior to energization, conductor tension shall be verified by sag chart comparison at 75°F reference temperature. Hi-pot testing of OPGW continuity and OTDR baseline shall be completed and submitted to Helix Power Cooperative within 10 business days of stringing completion.
