## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines how Meridian Grid Services personnel report, classify, and investigate safety incidents at Substation Alpha. All incidents - including near-misses - must be reported to enable trend analysis and corrective action tracking in the MGS Safety Management System (SMS).

## 2. Incident Classification

| Class | Definition | Reporting Timeline | Investigation Lead |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Class 1 - Recordable | OSHA recordable injury/illness | Within 1 hour | Safety Director |
| Class 2 - First Aid | Minor injury, no recordable criteria | Within 4 hours | Site Safety Manager |
| Class 3 - Near Miss | No injury, potential for serious harm | Within 8 hours | Supervisor |
| Class 4 - Property | Equipment/property damage > $5,000 | Within 4 hours | Project Manager |

## 3. Reporting Procedure Steps

1. **Secure the scene** - do not disturb evidence except to render aid or prevent further harm.
2. **Notify** Site Safety Manager (555-0177) and Project Manager immediately for Class 1 and Class 4 events.
3. **Render first aid** using site first aid kit (locations: CB-01, yard trailer, north gate).
4. **Complete Initial Incident Report** (Form SAF-501) within timelines above; include photos, witness names, and weather conditions.
5. **Notify Helix Power Cooperative** liaison for any incident affecting cooperative property or schedule (contact: HPC Field Rep, 555-0142 ext. 302).
6. **Preserve scene** for Class 1 incidents until Safety Director releases it (minimum 24 hours).
7. **Root cause analysis** using 5-Why or fishbone method within 5 business days for Class 1 and 2; 10 days for Class 3 and 4.
8. **Corrective actions** assigned in SMS with owner, due date, and verification method; tracked to closure.
9. **Lessons learned** shared at weekly Substation Alpha safety meeting (Tuesdays, 07:00, yard trailer).

## 4. Retaliation Prohibition

Meridian Grid Services prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports an incident in good faith.
