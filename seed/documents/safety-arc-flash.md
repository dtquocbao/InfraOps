## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines how Meridian Grid Services conducts arc flash hazard assessments at Substation Alpha per NFPA 70E (2024 edition) and IEEE 1584-2018. All energized work requires a completed arc flash risk assessment before work begins.

## 2. Assessment Method

Engineering performs arc flash calculations using SKM PowerTools (model updated 2026-01-15). Labels are affixed to all panels, switchgear, and MCCs per NFPA 70E 130.5(H). Site-specific incident energy values for Substation Alpha are maintained in the Arc Flash Database (SharePoint: MGS-Safety/ArcFlash/SA-2026).

## 4. Field Protocol Steps

1. Obtain current arc flash label data for the specific equipment from the database before entering the arc flash boundary.
2. Confirm approach boundary distances: shock boundary (138 kV: 7 ft 6 in limited, 8 ft 6 in restricted) and arc flash boundary per label.
3. Verify PPE inspection date (maximum 12 months from manufacture for arc-rated garments).
4. Complete Energized Electrical Work Permit (EEWP, Form SAF-205) signed by qualified person and safety manager.
5. Use insulated tools rated for system voltage; maintain minimum 10-foot clearance from unprotected workers during live work.
6. Document any label discrepancies in the MGS safety portal within 24 hours.
