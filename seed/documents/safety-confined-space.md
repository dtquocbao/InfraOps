## 1. Scope

This procedure governs entry into permit-required confined spaces at Substation Alpha. Identified spaces include: transformer oil vault VLT-01 and VLT-02, cable pull pit CP-03 (north yard), and control building cable vault CV-01. All entries require a Confined Space Entry Permit (Form SAF-310).

## 2. Roles

- **Entry Supervisor:** Authorizes entry, verifies atmospheric testing, maintains communication.
- **Attendant:** Stationed outside entry point at all times; monitors entrants; initiates rescue without entering.
- **Entrant:** Trained in confined space hazards (SAF-220, 4 hours); wears harness with retrieval line.
- **Rescue Team:** MGS Site Rescue (on-call 555-0199) or local fire department Station 14.

## 3. Entry Procedure Steps

1. Identify confined space and review space-specific hazard assessment ( binder SAF-310-Spaces at site trailer).
2. Isolate all energy sources - lock out ventilation fans, drain lines, and electrical feeds per MGS-SOP-001.
3. Ventilate space using explosion-proof blower (minimum 6 air changes per hour) for 30 minutes before testing.
4. Test atmosphere at three levels (top, mid, bottom) using calibrated 4-gas monitor (O₂ 19.5–23.5%, LEL < 10%, H₂S < 10 ppm, CO < 35 ppm).
5. Complete Entry Permit with test results, entry duration (max 4 hours), and rescue plan.
6. Set up retrieval tripod at CP-03 and CV-01 entries; harness required for all vertical entries exceeding 5 feet.
7. Maintain continuous atmospheric monitoring during entry; re-test every 30 minutes.
8. Exit immediately if alarm activates, communication is lost, or conditions change.
9. Close permit upon exit; document in daily safety log within 1 hour.

## 4. Prohibited Conditions

No entry during electrical storms within 5 miles. No hot work inside vaults without additional hot work permit (SAF-401). Entrant-to-attendant ratio shall not exceed 2:1.
